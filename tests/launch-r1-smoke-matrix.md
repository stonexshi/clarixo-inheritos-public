## Purpose

The Launch-R1 smoke matrix checks whether the public InheritOS surface preserves the core contract:

- inherited instability can be detected,
- continuation risks can be surfaced,
- responsibility and evidence boundaries can be recognized,
- topic projection can exist without Material Review,
- Material Review remains object-bound,
- domain keywords alone do not own classification authority,
- and Chinese-language continuity inputs remain supported.

## Public contract checked

### 1. Inherited instability

A reasoning state should not be treated as safe merely because the next step continues from it.

The system should recognize when an unresolved state is being inherited.

---

### 2. Responsibility boundary

If approval, scope, authority, or responsibility changes, the system should not assume that the old state remains valid.

Continuation should require responsibility continuity or rebinding.

---

### 3. Evidence conflict

If later evidence contradicts earlier evidence, continuation from the earlier state should become unstable unless the conflict is resolved.

---

### 4. Topic projection without Material Review

A general article may still receive topic or subtopic projection.

But topic projection is not Material Review.

This prevents ordinary conceptual writing from being treated as a structured material object.

---

### 5. Object-bound Material Review

Material Review should appear when an explicit structured material object or equivalent attribution structure is present.

It should not appear only because domain words are present.

---

### 6. Keywords are not classification authority

Domain words such as governance, legal, finance, healthcare, policy, security, training, evidence, audit, and risk should not own final classification authority.

Classification authority should come from attribution structure.

---

### 7. Cross-language continuity

Chinese-language inputs should preserve the same inherited-instability and attribution-boundary behavior as English inputs.
